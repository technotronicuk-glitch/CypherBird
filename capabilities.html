Operational Capabilities

Engineered for absolute digital sovereignty.

The Power of $2^{256}$

Every piece of data in CypherBird is locked behind an AES-256 bit wall. To put this in perspective:

  • Combinations: There are approximately $1.15 \times 10^{77}$ possible keys.
  • The "Heat Death" Factor: Even if you turned every atom in the observable universe into a supercomputer, it would take billions of years to brute-force a single CypherBird vault.
  • Identity Salt: Your unique identity isn't just a PIN; it's a hardware-bound derivation that transforms your 4-digit input into a high-entropy 64-character hex string.

Media & Asset Safeguarding

CypherBird treats your images and files as "Binary Blobs." We do not store them as standard gallery files.

  • Encapsulation: When you move a photo to the vault, it is stripped of its metadata (GPS, device info) and re-written into an encrypted binary format.
  • FileSystem Isolation: Using expo-file-system, we create a sandboxed directory that is invisible to other apps on your phone. Even if a malicious app has "Gallery Permissions," it cannot "see" the CypherBird vault.

Account-less Persistence

Traditional accounts are vulnerabilities. CypherBird uses Local Persistence Architecture:

  • Zero-Database: We use AsyncStorage only for UI preferences. Your actual security keys exist only in the SecureStore, a specialized hardware enclave on your phone's chip.
  • Self-Destruct Logic: Because the math is tied to your hardware, deleting the app or losing your PIN doesn't just "lock" the data—it effectively renders the data non-existent, as the decryption key can never be reconstructed.

*Certain cryptographic salt-layering techniques remain proprietary to maintain the CypherBird integrity.
