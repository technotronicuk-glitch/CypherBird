The Illusion of Privacy

Why standard encryption isn't enough anymore.

The Growing Risk

Every "secure" app you use today relies on a central server. This means your data—even if encrypted—is sitting in a cloud vault owned by a corporation. Data brokers, accidental leaks, and state-level surveillance don't "break" encryption; they simply demand the key from the provider.

Metadata Harvesting

They might not read your message, but they know who you talked to, when, and for how long.

Cloud Vulnerability

Backups are the "backdoor" of the 21st century. If your chat is backed up to a cloud, it is no longer private.
