Common Inquiries

Knowledge Base / Protocol Documentation

What exactly is Cypherbird?

Cypherbird is a decentralized sovereignty protocol designed to provide users with encrypted communication and private asset management, free from central oversight.

How does the "Eagle" protocol work?

It utilizes peer-to-peer mesh networking and end-to-end encryption. Your data never touches a central server; it "flies" between nodes until it reaches its destination.

Is my data really private?

Yes. We use zero-knowledge architecture. This means even the developers of Cypherbird cannot access your keys, messages, or files.

How do I get an Access Key?

Access keys are generated locally on your device during the Portal initialization. We never store these keys on any external drives.
